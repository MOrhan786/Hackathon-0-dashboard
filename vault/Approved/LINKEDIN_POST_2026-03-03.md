Building an AI Employee taught me something counterintuitive: 🧠

The most valuable features aren't the AI parts.

The HITL approval workflow, the audit logs, the DEV_MODE safety flag —
these "boring" infrastructure pieces are what make the AI trustworthy.

Anyone can call an LLM API. Shipping something you'd actually trust
with your emails, posts, and finances? That's the hard part.

My 40-hour hackathon project has more safety infrastructure
than most production AI tools I've seen.

What safety features do you consider non-negotiable in AI systems?
